FORECAST: ‘The Devil Wears Prada 2’ Suits Up for Summer

by Box Office Jedi

Thursday, April 30, 2026

Some say The Mummy (1999) inaugurated the first weekend of May as the summer movie kickoff weekend; others trace the tradition back to Twister in ’96 and Deep Impact in ’98. Spider-Man’s reality-shattering $114 million opening in 2002—the first opening weekend to surpass $100 million—solidified it as the tentpole weekend of the year.

The past two years have shown that the weekend isn’t all that significant anymore. The Fall Guy took it in 2024 with $27.6 million; Thunderbolts opened summer last year with $74 million. The slippage fits with a broader loss of seasonal markers. Weather patterns no longer follow any clear logic. New York is now subtropical. March and April serve up 35-degree days and 85-degree days in equal measure. When the calendar itself stops keeping time, marking the beginning of summer starts to feel beside the point.

But we return to a sense of normalcy this year with the opening of The Devil Wears Prada 2. At last, the first weekend of May feels like the first weekend of May, in the traditional sense, and summer is getting a real, true kickoff again.

What’s interesting here is the change in tune. Summer Starter weekends have historically featured a masculine-leaning blockbuster, often accompanied by a female-leaning counter-programming title. Think X2: X-Men United alongside The Lizzie McGuire Movie ($85.5M and $17.3M); Iron Man and Made of Honor ($98.6M and $14.8M); Wolverine and Ghosts of Girlfriends Past ($85.0M and $15.4M). And if there wasn’t a romantic comedy that same weekend, there’d be one on the calendar for the week after. This year there’s no other new film going up against Prada 2, though Mortal Kombat II will offer counter-programming next weekend.

It’s a long time coming. In 2006, The Devil Wears Prada debuted at #2 with $27 million. At #1 was Superman Returns, also debuting, which opened with $52.5 million ($84.6 million over five days). At the time, Prada was the counter-programming. Now it’s the headliner.

James Gunn’s Superman reboot came out last summer, opening to $125 million. While forecasts for Prada 2 are in the $95–105 million range, we’d bet that if Prada 2 had opened the same weekend as last year’s Superman—a sort of IP rematch—Prada might’ve ended up on top. That’s where our sights are set for this week’s predictions.

But unlike the average Summer Starter weekend, The Devil Wears Prada 2 won’t be soaring up there alone. May Summer Starter weekends usually look like a thumbtack in profile: a wide head, a thin pin. The #1 film accounts for something like 90% of the entire Top 20. Not this year.

Michael will hit hard in its second weekend. It defied critics and forecasts in its opening (industry forecasts ranged from $65 to $85 million), ending up at $97 million—surprising even our own $88.5 million projection, which we’d thought was on the high side. Weekday grosses have held strong, fans are turning out and dancing in the aisles, and many will be back. Once again, the closest comp feels like The Passion of the Christ, whose loyal fanbase spread the word and came back for more communion. That movie fell 36.5% in its second weekend (skewed a touch by its 5-day opening frame), and we’re predicting a similar hold for Michael—pretty much unheard of for a fan-driven blockbuster.

Welcome back to summer.
